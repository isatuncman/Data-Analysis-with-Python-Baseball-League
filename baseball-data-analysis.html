Baseball Data AnalysisÂ¶

This study investigates the baseball dataset and then communicate the findings about it. I use the Python libraries NumPy, Pandas, and Matplotlib to make the analysis easier. This project is a required part of Udacity Data Aanalysis Nano Degree Program.

Database:Â¶

"The database contains pitching, hitting, and fielding statistics for Major League Baseball from 1871 through 2016. It includes data from the two current leagues (American and National), the four other "major" leagues (American Association, Union Association, Players League, and Federal League), and the National Association of 1871-1875.

The database is comprised of the following main tables:

MASTER - Player names, DOB, and biographical info

Batting - batting statistics

Pitching - pitching statistics

Fielding - fielding statistics"

Sean Lahman Baseball Database

ConclusionÂ¶

The baseball data set is really huge and several more investigations may be made. Two important conclusion was that there exist huge differences between salaries. Although lower paid players do not differentiate within each other a lot, there exist huge variance within highest paid players. Another important implication was that it seems that there does not exist a direct relationship between high cost teams and their winning rates.

I have only used descriptive statistics in this study. Therefore, I did not test the statistical significance. However, we need to make hypothesis testing using z-test (since we have all population data on hand) to test the hypothesis that 'there exist a sgignificant relationship between paid salaries and winning rates of the teams each year'.

Correlation does not imply causation. So, we cannot claim that low salaries caused high winning numbers or vice versa. For future studies, the relationship between salaries of the players and their performances on specific positions (batting, fielding, pitching), the factors may have influence on the winning rate of a team can be investigated.

An important limitation for this study was that there exist limited data for salaries of Players. Furthermore, we do not have any information about extroneous factors occured (ex: World Wars, financial crisis etc) and impact of those factors. Another limitation is that the data is historical and we cannot make an experimental study. Our analyses are either ex-post facto or correlational. Therefore, we cannot investigate the causations of specific events.
